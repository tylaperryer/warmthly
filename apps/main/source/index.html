Source & Development

Transparency about how Warmthly was built, the standards we've strived to meet, and our current development status.

Warmthly has been built with a commitment to world-class standards across accessibility, security, performance, SEO, and privacy. This page shares our development journey, the challenges we've faced, and our current limitations.

Standards We've Strived to Meet

Accessibility Standards:

We've worked to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA and WCAG 2.2 Level AA standards across all pages. This includes proper semantic HTML, ARIA attributes, keyboard navigation, screen reader compatibility, multiple reading levels (Standard, Simplified Grade 6, Easy Read), multimedia accessibility with sign language videos, audio descriptions, and transcripts, and support for assistive technologies.

Security Standards:

We've implemented protections against OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities, comprehensive security headers (CSP, HSTS, X-Frame-Options, etc.), input validation and sanitization, secure authentication practices, and privacy-first architecture with no tracking or analytics.

Performance Standards:

We've optimized for Core Web Vitals, ensuring fast loading times, minimal layout shifts, and responsive interactions. Our site meets Google PageSpeed targets and follows performance best practices.

SEO Standards:

We've implemented proper semantic HTML, structured data (JSON-LD), Open Graph and Twitter Card tags, XML sitemaps, and follow Google Search Guidelines for optimal discoverability.

Privacy Standards:

We're committed to GDPR and CCPA compliance, with a privacy-first approach that includes no tracking cookies, no analytics, and transparent data practices.

Our Development Journey

The code for Warmthly was written using Cursor, an AI-powered code editor, over the course of 2 free trials spanning 2 weeks. This development process came with many headaches—navigating complex accessibility requirements, implementing security best practices, optimizing for performance, and ensuring cross-browser compatibility, all while learning and iterating on the fly.

Despite the challenges, we've worked hard to create a website that meets world-class standards across accessibility, security, performance, SEO, and privacy bars. Every feature, from reading level toggles to multimedia accessibility to security headers, was carefully considered and implemented to ensure the best possible experience for all users.

Current Status & Limitations

We currently do not have the funds to hire a professional developer nor to pay for Cursor again. As a result, the current website is static and will remain as such until funding arises.

This means that while we've built a solid foundation that meets many world-class standards, we're limited in our ability to add new features, make significant updates, or address complex technical issues without additional resources. We're transparent about this limitation because we believe in radical transparency about our operations, including our development constraints.

When funding becomes available, we plan to invest in professional development resources to enhance the site further, add new features, and continue improving our standards compliance. Until then, we're committed to maintaining the current static site and ensuring it continues to serve our mission of rehumanizing our world.

Open Source & Transparency

Our code is available on GitHub, and we welcome contributions, feedback, and collaboration. We believe in transparency not just in our operations, but in our code as well.

For detailed documentation about the standards we've implemented, please see our Standards Compliance documentation and other technical documentation in our repository.

Thank you for understanding our journey and our current limitations. We're committed to transparency, and we believe that sharing our development story—including the challenges and constraints—is part of building trust and authenticity.
